Accesibility Matters

Accessibility is about removing barriers to enable people to engage and take part in everyday activities.

In the work place this involves reducing and overcoming the barriers that might occur for people with disabilities and includes the digital and physical interactions that staff have in the workplace.

This does not imply solely wheelchair access ramps, nor does it mean just having braille signs at a business.

Some disabilities hidden and not immediately obvious, such as learning difficulties, mental health as well as mobility, speech, visual or hearing impairments. Working with a hidden disability can make tasks more demanding, but it can be difficult for others to identify, acknowledge or understand the challenges staff face.

However, bringing these issues to light with an employer can be a daunting idea that many fear will lead to their relationships with co-workers will changing, their employer seeing them as lazy or less capable, or that their career progress will stall as a result.

What We Do

Along with our partners, SODA Social, we have created a safe space where employees can anonymously discuss accessibility issues that they face in the work place.

This anonymity is vital to ensure that employees feel safe enough to voice there needs without fearing repercussions.

Employees can either create their own discussion idea or contribute to one that has already been brought to attention.

And employees with no accessibility concerns can join in too. This way, they can be included in the conversation and learn how to work alongside people with disabilities to create an inclusive and welcoming work environment.
